MAE

McGill Architecture Exhibition

This exhibition celebrates the culmination of student work carried out in the final year (two terms) of the Master of Architecture professional degree. Each student proposes a self-directed project, framing clear architectural issues and suggesting appropriate means of analysis, design, and representation. Together the students explore, describe, represent, mediate, and politicize a range of issues, sites, and ideas. Most of all, their projects show a deep concern for the consequences of architectural engagement, expanding the possibilities for positive change.

Macdonald-Harrington Building, 815 Sherbrooke W. McGill Metro. No tickets needed.

With our sincere thanks to the partners and sponsors who made this exhibition possible.
