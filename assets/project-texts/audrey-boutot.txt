TITLE: Homeplace: Aging at the Rural Edge
ADVISOR: David Covo

Across Northwestern Europe, the almshouse has long provided a distinct form of housing: small, independent dwellings offered at below-market rent to individuals with limited financial means, often older residents with strong ties to their community. Almshouses continue to operate today both as historic institutions and as newly built developments. In many rural areas, they remain an essential, sometimes the only, housing option for seniors in need. The model fulfills essential functions: it offers modest, self-contained homes for people looking to downsize; it provides below-market rent under structured oversight and long-term management; and it allows older residents to remain safely housed in environments that support aging in place within a community-focused setting.

The intention of this project is to apply the social and practical core of the modern almshouse model within the rural Québec context. In Québec’s rural municipalities, populations are aging more rapidly than in urban centers, with a high proportion of seniors living on low incomes. In the MRC of Drummondville, for example, an average of 24.6% of residents aged 65 and over live below the low-income threshold, meaning nearly one in four seniors has fewer financial resources than what is considered necessary to meet basic needs. Most rural towns lack viable alternatives for older residents who either wish to downsize, can no longer safely remain in their homes, or cannot afford private retirement residences. As a result, rural seniors are often faced with displacement from their communities as they age. The project aims to provide accessible, affordable housing that enables them to age both in place and in community. By offering modest dwellings rooted in the local context, the project allows residents to remain within their communities, maintain their connections to familiar surroundings, contribute to local economies, and sustain their independence. In doing so, it addresses the growing pressures faced by rural municipalities as populations age, while contributing to a more inclusive and supportive environment for older residents.
