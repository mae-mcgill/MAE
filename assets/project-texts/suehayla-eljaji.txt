TITLE: Places for Fake People
ADVISOR: Theodora Vardouli

The contemporary digital condition is the product of a long historical process that intertwines the emergence of modern capitalism with evolving quantitative sciences, technologies of control, and computational media. These historical layers reveal the emergence of a regime where capitalism subsumes statistics, cybernetics, computing, software, and labor into a complex apparatus of knowledge production and social control. This multifaceted process reconfigures truth, reality, and agency under new algorithmic paradigms, wherein quantification and automation govern lived experience, even as the infrastructures of extraction and exploitation remain obscured.

The temporality of architectural practice, marked by the irreducible presence of the unbuilt and speculative, is inseparable from the epistemological conditions of a world increasingly dominated by algorithmic and computational mediation. By crafting anticipatory simulations, architects operate within a domain where the “fake” precedes and shapes the “real,” reflecting a deeper logic of constructed realities foundational to contemporary socio-technical and capitalist landscapes. Consequently, architecture becomes a site where algorithmic capitalism materializes, reproducing and renegotiating social order through simulation, anticipation, and symbolic production. This confluence of statistical knowledge, cybernetic control, software infrastructures, and invisible labor exposes a transformation in the conditions of reality itself; how reality is perceived, produced, and governed. The algorithmic order has become an active and pervasive force that shapes perception, and organizes power in unprecedented ways.

It is within this context that this project locates its critical inquiry. Moving beyond conventional understandings of technology as neutral or instrumental, it positions the contemporary digital condition as the site of a “fake world”, a constructed, capitalist-driven algorithmic formation that increasingly mediates and defines what we take to be real.
