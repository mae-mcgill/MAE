TITLE: Primordial Time: Framing the Forest in the Gault Reserve
ADVISOR: Martin Bressani

Beginning with a series of speculative readings, the thesis examines the discrepancies between universal historicity — the absence of permanence in a world shaped by relentless human will or progress — and perceived permanence under the appearance of persisting unpolluted natural beginnings: “that which confirms its continuation” (Louis Kahn).

It turns toward Mont St-Hilaire, a site that holds particular poetic and ecological potential—a place where ancient persistence and human intervention remain visibly entangled. The mountain’s forest is divided between a public sector and one of the last vestiges of intact maple—hickory forest in the St. Lawrence Valley, while the Poudrette Quarry carves a vast scar into the same landscape.

The proposal intensifies three contemplative experiences latent in this site:
- Rejection — exclusion from the inaccessible virgin forest
- Wonder — encountering the primordial, an untouched persistence
- Power / Guilt — confronting the primordial need to quarry

To stage these experiences, the intervention traces the existing paths along the edge of the preserved forest and solidifies them into architecture: a wall of massive quarry-cut stone, punctuated by towers, running along the boundary between the public and protected sectors — evoking fortification walls that mark the edge of the forbidden.

Three spatial conditions along this “wall” correspond to the three experiences. The occupied wall near the reserve’s entrance adds to the existing educational facilities on site while redirecting visitors back to the public sector. The ruined wall — surviving towers made accessible to the public while the wall veil is reduced to a path — encountered past the lake suggests the fall of this edge allowing visitors to immerse themselves in the primitive forest as it spills over the edge. The penetrated wall suspends the viewers over the quarry, provoking both awe at human achievement and unease at its destructive capacity.
